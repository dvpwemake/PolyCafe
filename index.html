About Us

Polypinion Cafe, a haven for independent thinkers. At Polypinion, we embody the ethos of individual thought, offering a unique experience encapsulated in our slogan: "Taste of Opinions." We exclusively source ethically harvested and traded coffee, respecting the principles of sustainability and fostering a harmonious coexistence between humanity and nature.

We proudly source the coffee beans that are selected and roasted by the 2022 World Barista Champion and the 2023 Australian Barista Champion, ensuring an unparalleled standard of excellence and consistency in every cup. Polypinion Cafe is not just a coffee destination; it's a celebration of independent minds, ethical choices, and the delightful harmony found in a finely brewed cup. Neftaly Fajardo

COLOMBIA

$42

Neftaly Fajardo Salazar has a farm in Inzá, Cauca, where his farm Finca La Estrella sits on about 3.4 hectares of land. The coffee is picked at full maturation by hand, and the cherries are sorted initially by floating them in a tank and removing the ones that bob to the surface, in a process called "balseo."

The coffee is fermented in its intact cherry for 18 hours in a hopper, then de-pulped and open fermented in a cement-and-ceramic fermentation tank for 36 more hours. The coffee is washed twice and spread in parabolic dryers for 10–18 days.

Konga

ETHIOPIA

$49.00

This coffee was produced by local smallholder producers in the Konga kebele, or village, in the Yirgacheffe growing area of Ethiopia and was processed at the Konga washing station.

Yirgacheffe is a picturesque region, with altitude ranges from 1,700 to 2,200 meters above sea level, providing the coffee plants with the perfect combination of temperature and rainfall, resulting in slow maturation and concentrated flavours.

Butegana

BURUNDI

$39

Farmers delivering to Butegana washing station cultivate coffee in superb conditions of high altitudes, sunny days and cool nights. The high-quality potential of these conditions is preserved through yeast inoculated processing that emphasizes the superb quality of cherry and produces a delicious coffee.

Burundi's coffee is produced almost entirely by smallholder farmers, and much of this small-scale production is of exceptional quality.
